Voice Conversion By Just Doing k-Nearest Neighbors

Authors: Anonymous submission to INTERSPEECH 2023

Abstract: Voice conversion models have improved in recent years, with newer methods enabling conversion to and from speakers unseen during training. However, the processes needed to achieve these results are increasingly complex, often making them harder to reproduce and interpret. In this work, we introduce kNN-VC -- a robust method for performing any-to-any voice conversion. The method is simple: given feature vector sequences from a SSL model for the source and reference utterance, we map each vector from the source to a vector from the reference using the k-nearest neighbors algorithm. We can apply this approach to unseen speakers or even unseen languages during inference. On the LibriSpeech benchmark, we demonstrate improved speaker similarity and comparable intelligibility to existing methods with both objective and subjective evaluations. kNN-VC shows that realistic any-to-any voice conversion can be achieved using the simple kNN algorithm.
